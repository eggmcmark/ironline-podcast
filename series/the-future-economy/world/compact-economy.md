# The Compact Economy — World Reference (v4)

## How It Works in 2126

The Compact is 36 years old as governing law. It is not new. It is the way things work. Most people under 40 have never known anything else.

### Money: EQU

Backed by real asset appreciation. You earn it by creating verifiable real-world value. It exists only inside the Ledger. No external exchange rate. Cannot be speculated on. This is the non-negotiable design principle.

In practice: a carpenter earns EQU for building. A farmer earns EQU for harvesting. A teacher earns EQU through the Commons tithe (less than builders, a perpetual argument). A Ghost-class entertainer earns... almost nothing. The system sees their work as real only if it produces something the Ledger can audit.

### Work: CapEx Boards

Nobody has a "job." People have skill portfolios and project histories. A skilled carpenter works CapEx-matched projects three or four days a week, Guild workshop time the rest. "Full-time" is archaic.

### Verification: The Value Ledger

Distributed record of every material transaction. Foundation Assessors — the most trusted professionals in the Compact — verify that what claims to be real, is real.

### Governance: Asset Stewardship Council

Organized by asset class (Land, Built, Productive Capacity, Commons), not geography. No income tax. All public revenue from 4% appreciation tithe.

### Social Classes

**Founders** — Top. Guild-certified across three domains. Meritocracy calcifying into dynasty.
**Keepers** — Middle. Most people. They live well.
**Ghosts** — Growing underclass. Work the system can't see. Culturally essential, economically invisible.

### Trade with the Outside World

Barter. Goods for goods. Negotiated through Trade Boards. Deliberately high-friction.

**Exports**: Guild-certified physical goods (premium provenance — "certified human-made").
**Imports**: Medical research, computational capacity, rare materials, cultural products.

EQU never crosses the border. The Free Zones' attempts at EQU convertibility are one of the world's deepest political tensions.

**Smuggling**: Financial instruments, not goods. Credit and derivatives crossing into Compact territory.

### What Works

Stability. Meaning. A genuine relationship between work and worth. No inflation. No debt traps. Craft as identity. Physical skill as the highest status.

### What Doesn't

Ghost-class invisibility. Hereditary Founder wealth. No credit (can't start from nothing). Underfunded Commons. Intellectual insularity — the Compact is so confident it stopped being curious. Paradigm blindness: the system monitors Compact assets and literally cannot see what's developing outside its frame.

## The Free Zones

Our economy, continued. Credit, derivatives, speculation — all legal. Multiple currencies. More liquid, more innovative, more volatile, more unequal. Recognizable to a 2025 person. The Free Zones are us, kept going.

Richer on average. More consumer choice. More cultural production. More anxiety. More people falling through gaps. The food is better (more culinary diversity). The inequality is visible (luxury next to poverty in a way the Compact doesn't produce).

## The Empty Zones

The space between. Infrastructure for 10 billion, inhabited by almost no one. Highways maintained by robots. Suburbs with mowed lawns and no people. Schools where the lights still work. Nature reclaiming at the edges.

Not post-apocalyptic. Post-populated. Eerie, beautiful, melancholy. The autonomous maintenance systems keep running because nobody told them to stop.

A few hundred thousand people live out here — scavengers, dropouts, loners, people who don't fit. They have informal economies based on trust and barter. No Ledger. No currency. Just people knowing each other.

## The AI Layer

Plumbing. Invisible. All logistics, scheduling, supply chain, compliance, health monitoring, construction planning, agricultural optimization. The AI layer is what lets 4.5 billion people use infrastructure built for 10 billion.

AI doesn't build, repair, grow, teach, care, create, govern, or assess. Those are human. Everything else is machine.

The Compact treats AI the way we treat electricity. This is its own kind of blindness.

## Demographic Collapse

The world is emptier than ours. Cities contracted. Districts vacant. More buildings than people. Hands are scarce. Elder care is massive and undervalued. The elderly remember crowds. The young have never seen them. Long-distance travel is rare. The world is more local than 2025.

What this creates: a civilization that feels spacious, quiet, slightly melancholy. A world with room to breathe and not quite enough people to fill it. The background hum is not scarcity but *absence* — the ghost of a larger world that contracted.
