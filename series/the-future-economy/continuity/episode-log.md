| 1 | The Box | 2026-04-03 | 6,812 | ~38 min | Kirin Saleh | Sorting her dead grandmother's apartment, Kirin finds a hidden box containing a pre-Compact authentication key and a cryptic note. A materials analyst by trade, she can't identify the object. An archivist traces it to the Rootstock Protocol — a pre-Compact equity system. The key points to coordinates 400 miles into the empty zone. Then she finds hidden letters in an unreadable language and a photograph of a young Adaeze, radiant and unrecognizable, in front of a building that shouldn't exist. |
| 2 | What She Left Behind | 2026-04-09 | 8,493 | ~47 min | Kirin Saleh (primary); brief close-third on Perun and Brigid | One long day in Sable River. Perun reveals he was a Rootstock witness himself in 2078 and recovers Adaeze's pre-Compact name: Adaeze Okonkwo. The hidden letters are in Tamsat, a reconstructed language used by some Rootstock communities. Brigid, Kirin's supervisor at the CapEx Board, releases her with grace. Tomas outfits her for the journey. At a dinner with friends, a casual "the empty zones are empty for a reason" reveals the Compact's paradigm blindness to Kirin for the first time. At The Bench — an old-timers' social club — Reva remembers Adaeze from a plateau settlement in 2081 and names its founder: Nnedi. The turn comes when Mira, a Ghost-class elder-care aide, tells Kirin about a song Adaeze used to sing in a language none of them knew — *the road is long, but there is water at the end of it.* Kirin understands that her grandmother was not hiding but keeping a watch. She packs, stops at Adaeze's empty apartment at dawn, and leaves. |
| 3 | The Frontier | 2026-04-19 | 7,132 | ~40 min | Dez (cold open); Kirin Saleh (primary) | Kirin rides a freight transport to Kesmark, the last Compact settlement, then crosses on foot into the depopulated zone. Over two days she walks through empty suburbs maintained by autonomous systems — mowed lawns, burning school lights, a deer in a cafeteria — and camps alone for the first time in her life. She reaches a scavenger settlement in a repurposed commercial strip and meets Dez, a former Free Zone logistics coordinator who has lived in the empty zone for eight years. Dez's community includes Seun (ex-Compact structural engineer), Tov (former Ghost-class musician), and Caramel (self-taught electronics specialist). After Kirin shows Dez the authentication key and the coordinates 400 miles east, Dez agrees to guide her: "Nobody goes east. There's nothing east. That's what makes it interesting." Tov gives Kirin the name Cleo — sixty miles ahead at a rest stop, the person who knows the country further east — with a passphrase: "the small musician sends her regards." They leave at midday. |
| 4 | Signal | 2026-05-07 | 7,139 | ~40 min | Fen (cold open, unnamed); Kirin Saleh (primary); Tomas and Amira (interlude) | Kirin and Dez travel four days east from the settlement, reaching Cleo at the highway rest stop — a woman who has been at the edge of the empty zone for eleven years and knows the Rootstock key by reputation. Cleo gives them the route south from the transit corridor and reveals she's been leaving supplies at a research campus ahead for three years, and that someone there left her a note: "The key works." That night, Kirin reads the Tamsat letters alone for the first time. They leave at dawn, cross the transit corridor, and follow the service road south. On the fourth afternoon they reach the campus — an open gate, a lamp burning in the communications building window. Inside, the key opens a locked cabinet of handwritten Rootstock registry backups (43 binders, hundreds of claims, 2071–2089 northwest corridor). Behind the server room, the key opens a hidden sub-level containing a communications array receiving daily encrypted data packets from across the Pacific — running for at least two years. A figure appears from an inner door: "How long have you had the key." The episode ends on their first exchange. |
