# Marcus Hale — The Founder

## Vitals
- **Born**: 1983, Brooklyn, NY
- **Died**: 2061 (age 78), in his workshop, finishing a bookcase for his granddaughter
- **Season 1 age**: 44–57
- **Occupation**: Custom millwork contractor, general contractor (high-end residential)
- **Workshop**: Converted commercial space, Crown Heights, Brooklyn
- **Family**: Widower. Daughter Elena (born 2014). Father alive in Season 1 (retired contractor).

## Physical
- Hands are the first thing you notice: scarred, callused, precise. He handles tools the way a musician handles an instrument — without thinking about it.
- Medium build, starting to thicken at the waist by his late 40s. Still strong. The kind of strong that comes from decades of lifting, not from a gym.
- Wears the same thing every day: Carhartt work pants, a thermal under a flannel, Red Wing boots that he resolves every two years.
- Salt-and-pepper beard, kept short. Reading glasses he loses constantly.

## Voice (speech patterns for dialogue)
- **Terse**. Rarely uses three words when two work. Complete sentences are for clients.
- **Concrete**. Thinks and speaks in materials, dimensions, processes. "That's a shim problem, not a design problem."
- **Questions are real**. When he asks something, he wants the answer. Doesn't ask rhetorical questions.
- **Humor is dry and situational**. Never tells jokes. The funny thing is always the situation, delivered deadpan.
- **Doesn't hedge**. Says "I don't know" rather than "it could perhaps be..."
- **Brooklyn cadence**. Not heavy accent, but the rhythm is there — clipped, forward-moving.
- **Rarely curses on-site**. Off-site, moderately.

## Psychology
- **Core trait**: He wants things to make sense. Not theoretically — physically, materially, structurally. When something doesn't make sense, he can't leave it alone.
- **Relationship to work**: Work is not what he does, it's how he knows things. He understands truth through the resistance of materials. A joint that fits is true. A wall that's plumb is honest. This is not metaphor for him — it's epistemology.
- **Relationship to money**: Complicated. Not greedy, not indifferent. He knows what things cost and what they're worth, and he's bothered that these are different numbers.
- **Relationship to ideas**: Suspicious of abstraction until it proves itself in practice. But when an idea survives contact with reality, he's the most devoted convert.
- **Flaw**: He is stubborn past the point of utility. He will spend four hours fixing something that could be worked around in twenty minutes, because the workaround is a lie.
- **Blind spot**: He does not fully understand what he started. He sees equity participation as good business. Nadia sees it as a new economic order. He's not wrong — he's just not seeing the whole board.

## Key relationships
- **Nadia Okoye**: Intellectual partner, something like a friend, something like more, never quite anything. He trusts her mind completely. He finds her exhausting. She is his best audience and his sharpest critic.
- **Elena**: His daughter, his legacy, his most complicated relationship. He raises her with his instincts. She will build with her ferocity. He watches her become someone harder than he is and doesn't know if that's his fault or his gift.
- **Danny**: His foreman. Twenty years together. Danny thinks in logistics; Marcus thinks in craft. They argue about everything and trust each other completely.
- **His father**: Retired contractor. A man of fewer words than Marcus, which is saying something. His approval is the rarest and most valuable commodity in Marcus's life.

## Arc (Season 1)
Pragmatic tradesman → reluctant innovator → man watching an idea outgrow him → father passing the work to his daughter

## Death
2061, age 78. A stroke, in his workshop, while finishing a bookcase he was building for his granddaughter. Clean. Worthy. He does not see the Compact become law. He does not need to.
