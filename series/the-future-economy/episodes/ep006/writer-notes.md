# Writer Notes — Episode 6: "What She Read"

## What I Was Going For

This is the decompression episode. Episodes 3–5 were dense with corridor mechanics, new factions, new faces, and revelation-stacking. Episode 6 is the exhale: two people walking south through unclaimed territory with what they found. The structural challenge was making that walk feel earned and populated rather than transitional.

The emotional spine is Kirin processing the log. I chose NOT to dramatize the reading itself (we'd already done that in Ep 5) — instead the log appears in fragments: specific entries surfacing in what she notices, a physical response to the 2104 entry, and the deliberately withheld content of that entry. The listener knows she read something and stopped. The content of that entry is not described. This is intentional. Whatever Adaeze wrote about the accident is private, and making it private is the episode's biggest act of respect for the character.

The recontextualization this episode delivers is the Tamsat letters: Adaeze's appointment was institutional, not purely personal. She was *chosen* by an organization. This reframes everything — the watch wasn't one woman's ethical stubbornness, it was a role with a structure behind it, obligations running both directions.

## Continuity Callouts

- The water management entries added to Kirin's night reading (2098 tracking data) retroactively document that Adaeze was independently tracking the junction failure risk long before the Outside's twelve-year-old report. Implications for the Compact's non-response: don't resolve yet.

- Renata's chit glyph identified as 'Okonkwo' initial. Closes thread from Ep 3. Opens the question of why Naila chose to mark it that way. Hold for Ep 12.

- Gilles: recurring asset. Brief reappearance Ep 13. Do not give him a larger role — his reliability is the point.

- The 2104 entry: deliberately not shown. Do not reveal its contents in any future episode.

## What I'm Worried About

The Fen interlude is brief. Mitigation: Fen is shown NOT lying — procedural compliance, not cleverness. That's harder to undermine.

The transitional zone walking is deliberately thin on incident. The compensation is that the log-reading revelations (water entries, the Naila entry, the 2101 entry about Kirin's mother) are the episode's real events, arriving through memory rather than dialogue.

## New Characters

**Gilles**: Fifties. Six years at the Meridian Trade District crossing. Does not take EQU. Non-judgmental. Formal register if he appears again — procedural formality as protection for everyone.

## Setting Up Ep 7

Kirin arrives knowing: (1) the dyers' building, second floor; (2) Dara's name; (3) the atlas is what she's bringing. She does NOT know what Dara will do with it, whether Dara knows the atlas exists, or whether word about the key has reached Trade District channels. Ep 7's engine: Kirin completing a transaction in a city where the rules of value are not the rules she grew up with, and discovering that Dara already knows more than she should.
