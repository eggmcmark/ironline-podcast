# Writer Notes — Episode 4: "The Curated Mile"

## What I Was Going For

The episode's engine is simple: get from Salt to the next handler east, and make that journey earn its place in the arc. The corridor itself is the antagonist — not in a dramatic way, but in the way that infrastructure that is smarter than you know is always an antagonist. Kirin's skill set (materials analysis, reading surfaces) is the active tool here for the first time. She arrives at the depot having already read the corridor correctly before anyone tells her anything about it.

Yusra was designed to be the first person in the show who treats Kirin as a problem rather than a guest. The chip negotiation is a transaction between two people who each have something the other needs and neither has illusions about it.

## The Three-Faction Reveal

The tripartite curation arrangement answers the "who maintains the corridor" question from Ep 3, and complicates it immediately. The Compact shadow crew and Outside crew maintain the road and say nothing about each other because acknowledgment requires each to justify its own presence. The Curator is the third thing — the one that uses the road rather than keeping it. Placing Kirin in the lineage of four corridor-crossers (one missing east, one turned around, Renata, now her) without drama: just a statement. The listener counts backward.

## The Key's Behavior

The key authenticates to hostile infrastructure without asking Kirin. The node three revelation — that it gave a longer authentication to a Curator tap — means she can't stop it from responding. She can only understand what it responds to. The key's silence on the sensor-free path is the new development: negative-space grid-reading.

## The Operative Interlude

Kept short and cold. Bureaucratic. *Request additional credentialed operator* not *send a team.* The Guild certification photo detail grounds the intelligence-gathering in a recognizable human moment.

## Dez's "Not Yet"

The most load-bearing moment for their relationship. She admits something she doesn't have to. It tells Kirin she has a threshold she's monitoring.

## The Harrow Creek Landing

Ended on approach. The bird going east — Dez reading it correctly — gives the listener confidence in Dez's skills while keeping the incoming contact a mystery. Episode 5 opens on whoever comes from east.

- One carrier missing east: seed again at Ep 7–8.
- Operative's partner: 72 hours inbound, should appear Ep 5–6.
