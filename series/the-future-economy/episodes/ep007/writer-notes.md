# Writer Notes — Episode 7: "Meridian"

## What I Was Going For

This episode is a delivery episode, and the risk with delivery episodes is that they feel like receipts — the protagonist hands something over, the recipient nods, and we move on. The antidote is to make the delivery itself the recontextualization. Kirin walks in thinking she's completing Adaeze's instruction. She walks out understanding that the instruction was load-bearing in a way she didn't know.

The EQU-convertibility reveal is the heart of the episode. I wanted it to land not as a political thriller exposition dump but as the natural shape of a thing Kirin could work out from parts she already had — she's a materials analyst, she reads surfaces. Dara is not revealing something hidden; she's assembling pieces Kirin has been carrying for two months. The conversation needed to feel like recognition, not lecture.

Dara was the voice challenge. She needed to sound nothing like anyone Kirin has met: not Dez's watchful withholding, not Fen's subject-dropping efficiency, not Naila's medical-precise clipping. I went for controlled precision with an undercurrent of dry practicality — a person who respects efficiency as a form of care, who says exactly what the situation requires, no more. The line about not being altruistic (she can't afford to sell it either) is the key to her — she picked that up from Adaeze, who chose her specifically because moral commitment alone was not stable enough.

The cold open in Dara's POV is the third time we've used an antagonist-or-adjacent POV for the cold open. I used it here for a slightly different purpose: to establish that the threat is active before Kirin arrives, and to give Dara a life and a perspective before we see her through Kirin's eyes. The market alert being the third in two months is the ticking-clock that the episode runs on.

## The Alliance Fracture

The infrastructure contact scene is where Dez's limits show. I worked hard to make her reason for not naming the contact sympathetic without making her warm about it — the family detail is real and sufficient, not a softening of Dez's character but an accurate description of why someone in her position draws the lines they draw. The fracture doesn't resolve. It just finds a stable position. That's the right shape for Ep 7 — we need more fracture before we need resolution.

## The Log Reading

The two log entries Kirin finds in the café and at night do significant work:
1. "The hold requires more people than there are. Find them or watch it fail." (2113) — this is Adaeze's one moment of frustration in the log, written into the void, and it reframes the entire watch as something built under constraint.
2. The Brigid entry (2122) — the reveal that Brigid is a Rootstock-adjacent placement. This is the setup for Ep 14 and needs to sit quietly. I did not belabor it. Kirin sits with it and we move on.

The Tomas entry (2118) is a plant. He knew. He saw Adaeze at Kesmark in 2118 and didn't approach. The corridor has known Tomas a lot longer than Kirin realizes.

## Continuity Callouts

- Atlas delivered: Adaeze's first instruction complete.
- The convertibility play is now fully named. The "third faction" that Yusra mentioned at Larch — the one Kirin couldn't identify — may be connected to the east financial district firm. Do not confirm this before Ep 11.
- The man in the lane is from the east financial district firm. He is NOT the Curator's operative. These are different threats with different motivations. Keep them separate.
- Key still dormant in Meridian. No Rootstock-compatible infrastructure here. The key will not activate again until the right room — which is the Outside, Eps 9-10.
- Dez's infrastructure contact is unnamed deliberately. The contact's family is a genuine constraint, not a red herring.

## What I'm Worried About

The episode runs a little conversation-heavy in the Dara section. The physical grounding (the follower in the lane, Kirin at the window, Dara placing the atlas on the shelf) helps, but a future revision might add one more physical action beat in the middle of the Dara conversation to break it up.

The transition from café to lodging to night reading is fast. If the episode feels thin in pacing review, the evening section could be extended with more of the city's sensory texture.

The landing (Kirin looking at the window, settling her focus on what's further away) is subtle. It's meant to echo the key's own dormancy — both are waiting for something that speaks their language. Don't explain this in revision; if it doesn't land on first read, trust the audio version to carry it.

## New Characters

**Dara:** Capability broker, Meridian Trade District, second floor above the dyers. Mid-forties. Compact-born, Free Zone resident for fifteen-plus years. Has been Adaeze's Meridian contact since 2112. Three confirmed deliveries of partial population survey data. Now holds the complete atlas. Interests: keeping the convertibility play from succeeding (because success collapses the between-systems space she operates in). Price: one return contact per six months from Kirin. Speech: controlled precision, economical, no warmth performed or required, dry humor in the negative space. Reappear: Ep 12 (Salt operation involves Free Zone elements). Possible: Ep 13 (reverse transit).
